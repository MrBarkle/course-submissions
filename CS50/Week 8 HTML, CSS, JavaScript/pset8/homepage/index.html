While playing LOTRO I went by many names, faces, and titles, however, it was most common that I was simply referred to as Barkle by friends and enemies alike. Now I know what you must be thinking... friends? Enemies? In a video game? Well in some ways an MMO is more than just a video game; it’s a lifestyle, a part of who we are as players. For the 10 years I spent playing LOTRO I was a part of a vast community of players composed of varying skill levels and playstyles. This community consisted of familiar faces both acting as helpful allies and as competition in the battle for middle-earth. It was a digital world of questing and adventure but like any other society it was not without its own political, economic, and social systems. Over time allies became friends and the competition became the enemy. There were great leaders and loyal followers as well as opposing factions and waring competitors all attempting to coexist at once. People came and went; some returned years later, others were never to be heard from again. The following links take a look inside some of my favorite identities and the roles taken on by each.
